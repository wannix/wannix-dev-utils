# Business Guidelines - Foodstuffs North Island

## Organization Overview

### Company Identity
- **Name**: Foodstuffs North Island (commonly referred to as "Foodies")
- **Type**: Homegrown Co-operative
- **Scale**: New Zealand's largest grocery retailer
- **History**: Operating since 1922 (over 100 years)
- **Footprint**: 300 stores and 24,000 employees across the North Island
- **Geographic Coverage**: From Four Square Houhora (Far North) to New World Island Bay (Wellington)

### Mission & Values
- **Core Purpose**: "To make sure New Zealanders get more out of life"
- **Strategic Vision**: Becoming the world's most customer-driven retailer
- **Community Commitment**: Being a positive force for New Zealand
- **Ownership Model**: Every store is locally owned and operated by independent grocers
- **Local + Scale**: Each grocer operates locally while leveraging organizational scale

## Brand Portfolio

### Store Brands
1. **PAK'nSAVE** - Value-focused supermarkets
2. **New World** - Premium grocery experience
3. **Four Square** - Community convenience stores
4. **Gilmours** - Foodservice and wholesale
5. **Liquorland** - Licensed liquor retail

### Own Brands
- **Pams** - Private label products
- **Social Supermarkets** - Community-focused retail initiative

## Key Business Principles

### Customer Experience
- Committed to delivering "awesome experiences for our customers everyday"
- Each grocer tailors offerings to local customer needs and communities
- Balance between local autonomy and organizational consistency

### Community Responsibility
- Grocers have a responsibility to be a positive force in their communities
- Active community support (e.g., keeping stores open during emergencies like flooding)
- Partnership with organizations like Kaibosh for food rescue and redistribution

### Transparency & Accountability
- Full support for all market study recommendations
- Commitment to grocery market regulation compliance
- Public reporting through:
  - Annual Reports
  - Social and Sustainability Reports
  - Grocery Market Regulation Dashboard

## Development Priorities

When building applications or features for Foodstuffs North Island:

1. **Prioritize customer experience**: Every feature should tie back to making shopping easier/better
2. **Enable local autonomy**: Tools should allow store owners to customize while maintaining brand consistency
3. **Support scale efficiently**: Solutions must work across 300 stores
4. **Community integration**: Consider local impact and community value
5. **Transparency**: Make information accessible and trackable
6. **Sustainability**: Consider environmental impact of technical decisions
7. **Mobile-first**: Customers and employees need mobile access
8. **Accessibility**: Ensure inclusive design for diverse New Zealand communities
